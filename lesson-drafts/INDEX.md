# Lesson Drafts — Ilu's School of Thoughts

This directory holds the first-pass drafts of the school's eighteen lesson essays, organized by tier. Each file is a self-contained Markdown document with YAML frontmatter, a lede, three numbered sections, a pull quote, footnotes, and a closing flourish. The drafts are written in a shared house voice — literary-philosophical, patient, slightly elegiac — and are intended to be polished, fact-checked, and edited for production. Treat each as a working draft, not a finished essay.

## Known issues

The drafts have been reviewed across three dimensions — citation integrity, voice consistency, and structure. Voice and structure passed cleanly across all eighteen drafts; citation integrity was largely clean but surfaced two findings that warrant editorial attention before publication, plus a small set of low-severity notes.

### Citation integrity — items needing author attention

**Medium severity**

- **tier-I-course-iv-ethics-without-eternity.md** — The lede frames Ivan Karamazov's "if God does not exist, everything is permitted" as a proposition delivered to Alyosha in a tavern in the spring of 1880. In the actual novel the line does not appear verbatim in that exact formulation, and the famous formulation is paraphrased reported speech rather than Ivan's direct statement to Alyosha; the framing conflates several scenes. Consider rewording to acknowledge the line as the reception-history paraphrase it has become.

- **tier-III-course-xvii-capstone-methodology-and-defense.md** — The pull quote attributed to Booth, Colomb & Williams, *The Craft of Research* ("A topic becomes a problem only when you can show your reader why not knowing the answer costs them something") is presented as verbatim attribution but is in fact a paraphrase of their argument with no exact textual source. Either soften the attribution to "after Booth, Colomb & Williams" or replace with a genuine direct quote from the book.

**Low severity** (defensible but worth a final pass)

- **tier-I-course-i-the-word-atheism.md** — Footnote 2 dates Russell's "Is There a God?" to Vol. 11 of the Collected Papers; verify the specific volume designation.
- **tier-I-course-ii-brief-history-of-doubt.md** — Hecht's *Doubt: A History* is cited as "HarperOne, 2003"; the original 2003 imprint was HarperSanFrancisco, with HarperOne the rebranded name from 2007 onward. Date is correct; publisher-name is a minor anachronism.
- **tier-I-course-iii-reason-evidence-examined-belief.md** and **tier-II-course-viii-justified-belief-and-the-ethics-of-evidence.md** — Clifford pull quote is given with an added comma after "wrong"; close enough to count as direct but worth noting.
- **tier-II-course-viii-justified-belief-and-the-ethics-of-evidence.md** — Lede dates Clifford's essay to 1876 (delivery to the Metaphysical Society) while footnote 1 dates publication to 1877 (Contemporary Review). Both defensible; tension is minor.
- **tier-I-course-extra-why-this-school-exists.md** — The Mill pull quote is internally labeled "paraphrasing Mill" but is actually a verbatim line from *On Liberty* ch. II. Remove the "paraphrasing" tag.

### Voice consistency — clean

All eighteen drafts share a consistent house voice. No AI tells, no triumphalism, charitable reading of theists throughout, instructor tonal variation holds together as one school. No issues detected.

### Structure — clean

All eighteen drafts meet the structural requirements (lede, three numbered sections with Roman numerals and two paragraphs each, pull quote with attribution, three to four footnotes, ∴ closing flourish, word counts within range). No issues detected.

### Overall assessment

The corpus is largely clean. No invented titles, no fabricated authors, no fundamentally wrong dates. Two medium-severity items (the Karamazov framing and the Booth/Colomb/Williams pull quote) should be addressed before publication; the low-severity items are stylistic or near-the-line and can be addressed in normal editing.
