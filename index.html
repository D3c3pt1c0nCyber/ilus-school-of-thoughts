The debate-first idea

You don’t understand a view until you can argue it well.

Most introductions to atheism give you a list of arguments and rebuttals. Ilu’s School inverts that. Content is the raw material; the skill is the product.

You’ll learn to reconstruct an opponent’s argument in its most charitable form, locate its true load-bearing premise, and respond with logic rather than slogans — making you harder to fool, slower to strawman, and more persuasive precisely because you argue fairly.

The core loop

Learn → Drill → Spar → Reflect

Every lesson cycles you through four phases. Read the idea, drill the micro-skill in isolation, apply it under pressure against a peer, then review against a rubric.

1

Learn

Short readings, narrated video, and primary-source excerpts with inline notes.

2

Drill

Fallacy spotting, argument mapping, and spaced-repetition recall — practised in isolation.

3

Spar

Structured exchanges against a peer or mentor in the Debate Arena and Socratic Circles.

4

Reflect

Rubric-based feedback, a recording to review, and a six-dimension skill radar.
